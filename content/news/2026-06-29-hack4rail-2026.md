### Hack4Rail 2026: Advancing Rail Innovation Through Open Collaboration

For its seventh edition, Hack4Rail brought the European railway community together in Vienna. Around 160 participants from leading railway organisations across Europe joined Hack4Rail 2026 to work on real challenges and develop concepts and prototypes for the future of rail. Hosted at the ÖBB Open Innovation Factory under the theme “Connected Railways for Open Solutions”, the event brought together expertise from ÖBB, Deutsche Bahn, SBB CFF FFS, Infrabel, Groupe SNCF, NS and the OpenRail Association.

The OpenRail Association played an active role by contributing challenges such as Build an Open Model of Cross-Border Mobility, Infrastructure Congestion, PopUpSim Optimizer, Robust Auto-Layout Algorithms for the Netzgrafik Editor and Rolling Stock Software Configuration Management. Through its welcome words and interviews, it also highlighted the value of sharing expertise, combining railway and IT knowledge, and enabling open source collaboration beyond the event through infrastructure and direct support.

### Accelerating Innovation for the Future of Rail

Following one and a half days of focused collaboration and development, 22 teams presented solutions for some of the rail sector’s most pressing challenges. The pitches showed how quickly strong ideas can become tangible prototypes when operational expertise, data, technology and a shared passion for rail come together. The teams addressed topics ranging from infrastructure planning and freight congestion to disruption management, station navigation, energy forecasting and passenger support. Across the presentations, a clear focus emerged on reducing complexity, increasing transparency and improving decision-making.

### From Shared Challenges to Open Source Collaboration

Hack4Rail’s “Rolling Stock Software Configuration Management” challenge highlighted a strategic opportunity for the railway sector: turning shared software challenges into structured collaboration. Bringing together peers from SNCF, DB, ÖBB and NS, the challenge created a common understanding that operators are facing the same issues in train software management. According to insights shared by Julien Spanneut, the combination of rolling stock expertise and IT capabilities proved especially powerful, showing that existing open-source solutions can offer a viable path forward. In less than one day, participants designed a minimum valuable product. “The next meaningful step would be to convince decision makers to officially start a common open source project among railway operators,” Julien Spanneut said, pointing to the potential for deeper collaboration across the railway sector and to the value of establishing a network of train software experts to exchange regularly and jointly advance the topic.

### From Hackathon Energy to Business Impact

In his inspiring closing words, Jochen Decker reflected on how Hack4Rail has become a powerful platform for turning collaboration, technology, and business ambition into tangible impact. He emphasized that the latest edition showed how international collaboration, business ownership, and AI can accelerate innovation in the rail sector. He also highlighted that AI played a key role by supporting development, analysis, and delivery across the teams. Participants turned highly relevant business challenges into working prototypes within only a few days, with results that were more mature, more practical, and closer to real-world implementation than in previous editions. At the same time, he underlined that AI must be used responsibly, ensuring that speed and innovation do not come at the expense of quality, security, or personal well-being. He also pointed to the growing international relevance of collaboration across the rail industry: rather than competing with each other, railways should join forces to increase the attractiveness and performance of rail as a whole. His main message was that real value is created only when promising prototypes and concepts are followed up and brought into production. Business owners and technology teams are now called upon to turn the strongest concepts into measurable impact. Hack4Rail proved that silos can be overcome when international teams across the rail industry work with a shared purpose, combine their expertise, and maintain a clear focus on customer value.

### Netzgrafik Editor Improvement Wins Second Place

Team "Click Magic" was awarded second place for its impressive contribution to an OpenRail Association challenge on robust auto-layout algorithms for the Netzgrafik Editor. Congratulations to the team on this well-deserved achievement. As rail networks become more complex, Netzgrafik readability can quickly deteriorate, often requiring extensive manual rework. The team developed an approach that preserves the network topology while automatically improving visual clarity. Their “one click magic” prototype transforms dense and hard-to-read network data into a clearer, more usable layout within minutes, helping planners reduce manual effort and work more efficiently with complex and evolving rail networks.

### Open Collaboration as a Catalyst for Rail Innovation
Hack4Rail 2026 once again demonstrated that open collaboration can turn shared challenges into practical solutions for the rail sector. The event showed the strength of combining operational knowledge, digital expertise and open-source principles across organisational borders. As the most promising prototypes now move toward follow-up and implementation, Hack4Rail continues to be an important catalyst for a more connected, innovative and customer-focused railway future.
