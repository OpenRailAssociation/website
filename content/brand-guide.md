This brand guide defines the visual identity of the OpenRail Association. It ensures consistency across all materials and makes it easy for contributors to apply the brand correctly.

### Logo

The OpenRail Association logo is available in three layouts, each in color, noir (black), and blanc (white) variants. All logo files have a transparent background.

#### Square format

For social media, icons, and square placements. Logo mark above, text below.

{{< logo-table section="square" >}}

#### Wide format

For headers, documents, and horizontal placements. Logo mark integrated as the "O".

{{< logo-table section="wide" >}}

#### Icon mark

For favicons, app icons, and small-space usage. The circular rail mark without text. This file has a white background (not transparent).

{{< logo-table section="mark" >}}

#### Usage rules

- Always maintain clear space around the logo equal to at least the height of the "O" in "OpenRail"
- Do not stretch, rotate, or distort the logo
- Do not place the logo on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Do not alter the logo colors or add effects

### Key messages

**What is OpenRail?**

> The OpenRail Association provides a neutral space for collaborative development of Open Source software in the railway sector. It is incorporated as a non-profit organisation according to Belgian law as AISBL.

**Short version (one sentence):**

> OpenRail is the non-profit association for Open Source collaboration in the railway sector.

**Core messages:**

- Open source delivers production impact in railway operations, enabling cross-organizational collaboration and strengthening digital resilience
- Railways across Europe face shared challenges that require shared solutions; open source enables collaboration, innovation, and interoperability across borders
- Digital sovereignty cannot be achieved without open source; transparency, neutral governance, and collaborative development create the conditions for choice and resilience
- OpenRail connects member organizations and open source projects within a structured and reliable framework, open to the entire railway ecosystem
